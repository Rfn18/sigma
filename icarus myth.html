The Myth of Icarus

In Greek mythology, Icarus was the son of Daedalus, a skilled craftsman who created wings made of feathers and wax so they could escape from Crete by flying. Despite warnings from his father not to fly too close to the sun or too near the sea, Icarus became overconfident and flew too high. The sun’s heat melted the wax in his wings, and he fell into the sea and drowned. This myth is often seen as a cautionary tale about hubris and the dangers of overambition.
